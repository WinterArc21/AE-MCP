# Storytelling Through Motion — AE Technique Reference

---

## Hook / Build / Climax / Resolve — Timing Percentages

| Phase | Time Range | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| HOOK | 0–15% | Grab attention. Strong visual, unexpected move, bold opening frame |
| BUILD | 15–60% | Introduce ideas, accumulate complexity. Pace increases |
| CLIMAX | 60–75% | Peak visual moment. Most complex, fastest, loudest, biggest |
| RESOLVE | 75–100% | Release tension. Slow down. Hold a final frame. Breathe |

## Scene Count Guidelines

A **scene** = a distinct visual space/concept separated by a transition.

| Duration | Recommended Scene Count | Avg. Scene Duration |
|---|---|---|
| 10 seconds | 2–3 scenes | 3–5s each |
| 15 seconds | 3–5 scenes | 3–4s each |
| 30 seconds | 5–8 scenes | 3.5–6s each |
| 60 seconds | 8–12 scenes | 5–7s each |
| 90 seconds | 12–18 scenes | 5–7.5s each |

Rule: Under 3 scenes = feels like a logo loop. Over 10 in 30s = feels like a highlight reel.

---

## Hold-to-Transition Ratio Table

| Energy Level | Scene Hold | Transition Duration | Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| Slow / cinematic | 3–6 seconds | 20–30 frames (0.8–1.2s) | ~5:1 |
| Standard motion design | 1.5–3 seconds | 10–15 frames (0.4–0.6s) | ~5:1 |
| High energy / music video | 0.5–1.5 seconds | 6–10 frames (0.25–0.4s) | ~4:1 |
| Extreme / fast cut | 0.2–0.5 seconds | 3–5 frames | ~3:1 |

**Rule of thumb:** Transitions = 15–25% of scene total duration.
- 2s scene → 0.3–0.5s transition
- 5s scene → 0.75–1s transition

## Pacing Decision Rules

**Speed up when:**
- Introducing multiple concepts quickly (information burst)
- Matching high-energy section of music (rising phrase, beat drop buildup)
- Creating tension before a payoff
- Repeating visual patterns the viewer already understands

**Slow down when:**
- Delivering the core message
- After a climax moment (viewers need to process)
- Showing detail requiring reading time (data viz, product specs)
- Targeting luxury / premium brand feel (luxury = always slower than average)
- Adding breathing room — one slow hold in a fast piece creates disproportionate impact

**Contrast principle:** Fast sequences feel fastest immediately after a slow hold. Design with contrast — never uniform speed throughout.

**Frame count timing intuition:**
- 4-frame transition = abrupt, energetic (ping-pong ball)
- 6–10 frame transition = cinematic sweet spot
- 20-frame transition = weighty, considered (bowling ball)

## Motion Hierarchy — 5 Decisions (in order)

When designing any scene, decide in this sequence:

1. **Timing** (how long?) — driven by music BPM and narrative structure
2. **Motion direction** (which way?) — driven by visual flow and composition rules
3. **Easing** (how does it start/stop?) — driven by the object's "weight" and emotional tone
4. **Scale and speed** (how big / how fast?) — driven by energy level at that story moment
5. **Visual complexity** (how many things moving?) — driven by cognitive load and pacing

**Cognitive load limit:** Viewers track ~3 independently moving elements at once. 4th+ elements must be background/texture — not demanding attention.

---

## Visual Flow — Exit/Enter Direction Continuity

**Core rule:** Every transition preserves momentum. The exit velocity at the cut is maximized via steep ease-out curves.

| Object exits → | Next scene enters from ← |
|---|---|
| Right | Left |
| Left | Right |
| Up | Down |
| Down | Up |
| Zoom in (camera pushes) | Zoom continues, or zoom out resolves |
| Rotate clockwise | Clockwise continues |

**Breaking the rule** = intentional jarring cut. Use only for: shock moments, hard beats, deliberate disorientation.

## 3 Structural Templates

### 15-Second (social ad, logo ident)

```
:00–:02  HOOK        Opening visual hit or reveal. No text yet.
:02–:07  INTRODUCE   Main concept/product. 2–3 scenes.
:07–:11  BUILD       Supporting points or features. Fastest pacing.
:11–:13  CLIMAX      Logo or hero shot with full motion energy.
:13–:15  HOLD        Final frame, tagline fades in. Minimal SFX.
```

- 4–5 scenes total
- Max 1 transition per 2.5s average
- Last 2 seconds always slower than rest

---

### 30-Second (product launch, explainer)

```
:00–:03  HOOK        Single powerful opening image. Brand color hits.
:03–:08  PROBLEM     Context or challenge. 1–2 scenes.
:08–:18  SOLUTION    Main message/product. 3–4 scenes, increasing energy.
:18–:24  CLIMAX      Peak moment. Fastest pacing, most visual complexity.
:24–:28  BENEFIT     Key takeaway or CTA. Pace slows.
:28–:30  END CARD    Logo, tagline, URL. Static or near-static hold.
```

- 6–8 scenes total
- Climax section feels 40% faster than opening
- End card: minimum 2 seconds of near-stillness (ideal: 3 seconds)

---

### 60-Second (brand film, product overview)

```
:00–:05  HOOK
:05–:15  CONTEXT / WORLD-BUILDING
:15–:30  FEATURES / STORY      Multiple scenes, mid-pace
:30–:45  CLIMAX SECTION        Fastest pacing, full visual complexity
:45–:55  RESOLUTION            Slower, more contemplative
:55–:60  END CARD              Static hold, logo reveal
```

- 10–14 scenes total
- Two "breathing" moments: ~:15 (after opening energy) and ~:45 (before end)
